  <h1 class="page-title">Papers</h1>

  <div class="papers-list">

    <div class="paper">
      <div class="paper-title"><a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2103.06712">A semi-agnostic ansatz with variable structure for quantum machine learning</a> <a class="pdf-link" href="#" onclick="openPdf('https://arxiv.org/pdf/2103.06712'); return false;">pdf</a></div>

      <div class="paper-desc">A variable structure approach that grows and removes gates to keep quantum circuits shallow. Mitigates trainability and noise issues in variational algorithms. Demonstrated on VQE, quantum autoencoders and unitary compilation.</div>
    </div>

      <div class="paper-title"><a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2001.10283">Real-time calibration of coherent-state receivers: learning by trial and error</a> <a class="pdf-link" href="#" onclick="openPdf('https://arxiv.org/pdf/2001.10283'); return false;">pdf</a></div>

      <div class="paper-desc">Reinforcement learning agent that calibrates adaptive quantum receivers from scratch. Learns near-optimal setups under trial-and-error episodes. Benchmarked against Helstrom bound and Kennedy/Dolinar receivers.</div>
    </div>

    <div class="paper">
      <div class="paper-title"><a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.09807">RL calibration of coherent-state receivers on variable-loss optical channels</a> <a class="pdf-link" href="#" onclick="openPdf('https://arxiv.org/pdf/2203.09807'); return false;">pdf</a></div>

      <div class="paper-desc">Extends RL-based receiver calibration to fading channels with variable transmissivity. Shallow RL methods achieve near-optimal performance. Presented at the IEEE Information Theory Workshop.</div>
    </div>

    <div class="paper">
      <div class="paper-title"><a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.14954">Sequential hypothesis testing for continuously-monitored quantum systems</a> <a class="pdf-link" href="#" onclick="openPdf('https://arxiv.org/pdf/2307.14954'); return false;">pdf</a></div>
      <div class="paper-meta">G Gasbarri, M Bilkis, E Roda-Salichs, J Calsamiglia &middot; Quantum 8, 2024</div>
      <div class="paper-desc">Sequential analysis of streaming measurement data for quantum hypothesis testing. Stopping-time strategies outperform fixed-time approaches. Resource savings by a factor of 3-4 in optomechanical case studies.</div>
    </div>

    <div class="paper">
      <div class="paper-title"><a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1812.11251">Conditional states and entropy in qudit-qubit systems</a> <a class="pdf-link" href="#" onclick="openPdf('https://arxiv.org/pdf/1812.11251'); return false;">pdf</a></div>

      <div class="paper-desc">Analysis of conditional states and quantum correlations in hybrid qudit-qubit systems. Explores entropic measures of quantum discord. Published as part of the licenciatura thesis work at UNLP.</div>
    </div>

    <div class="paper">
      <div class="paper-title"><a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.10726">Automatic re-calibration of quantum devices by reinforcement learning</a> <a class="pdf-link" href="#" onclick="openPdf('https://arxiv.org/pdf/2404.10726'); return false;">pdf</a></div>

      <div class="paper-desc">RL-based automatic recalibration for drifting quantum devices. Supervised master thesis work awarded Mención Masperi AFA 2024. Best master thesis in Physics in Argentina.</div>
    </div>

    <div class="paper">
      <div class="paper-title"><a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.06416">Hybrid classical-quantum architecture for vectorised image classification of hand-written sketches</a> <a class="pdf-link" href="#" onclick="openPdf('https://arxiv.org/pdf/2407.06416'); return false;">pdf</a></div>

    </div>

    <div class="paper">
